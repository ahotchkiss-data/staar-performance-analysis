# Analyzing STAAR Performance: Trends and Gaps in a Texas Middle School

## Project Overview

This project analyzes middle school STAAR assessment data to identify trends in student performance, achievement gaps, and areas requiring targeted instructional intervention.

The dashboards were developed using Microsoft Power BI to support school administrators and instructional leaders in making data-driven decisions and allocating remediation resources more effectively.

---

## Data Source

The dataset was compiled from internally maintained academic performance spreadsheets exported into Excel format. The data included:

- Student grade level
- STAAR test results by subject
- Emergent Bilingual (EB) status
- Assigned remediation hours

The STAAR assessment results reflect student performance from the **2024–2025 academic year**. The students are represented in the dataset by their **current grade levels during the 2025–2026 academic year**, allowing analysis of prior assessment outcomes in the context of students’ current instructional placement.

All data were anonymized and handled in compliance with FERPA privacy regulations.

- Microsoft Power BI

---

## Data Cleaning & Preparation

The following data preparation steps were performed:

- Removed duplicate and empty student records to ensure data accuracy.
- Standardized grade level and subject naming conventions.
- Handled missing values in remediation and test result fields.
- Created calculated fields for total remediation hours per student.
- Combined multiple Excel files into a unified dataset.
- Validated consistency across grade levels and subject areas.

---

## Dashboard Design & Analysis

The dashboards were designed to provide both high-level and detailed, grade-specific insights for school leadership.

Key design features include:

- KPI cards displaying total students, failure counts, and remediation needs
- Bar charts showing failure rates by subject and grade level
- Pie charts illustrating EB population distribution
- Detailed tables displaying student-level remediation requirements
- Separate dashboards for overall performance and individual grade levels

These dashboards enable administrators to quickly identify performance gaps and prioritize intervention efforts.

## Key Insights

- Math consistently exhibited the highest failure rates across subjects, indicating a critical need for targeted intervention.
- 8th grade students showed particularly high math failure rates, highlighting a key risk area before high school transition.
- Science demonstrated the lowest failure rates among 6th grade students, suggesting relatively stronger performance.
- Emergent Bilingual students represented a disproportionate share of students requiring remediation, highlighting an opportunity for targeted instructional support.

---

## Impact and Application

These dashboards provide school leadership with a centralized, visual tool to:

- Identify high-risk students quickly.
- Prioritize remediation efforts.
- Allocate instructional resources effectively.
- Monitor academic performance trends across grade levels.

This enables faster, more informed decision-making to improve student outcomes.

---

## Scope and Limitations

This analysis is based on STAAR data from a single middle school in the Rio Grande Valley and is intended to identify internal performance trends rather than represent statewide performance. Insights should be interpreted within the context of this specific student population.

---
